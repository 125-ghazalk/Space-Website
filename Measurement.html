The sun: A unit of measurement?

Image of the sun

Astronomical Units (AU) are a unit of cosmic measurement, since the normal measurements we use in our every day lives will equal massive numbers when used in space. AUs are measured by the distance between the sun and Earth's orbit (not the center or surface). The distance is around 150 million kilometers. So, one Astronomical Unit is roughly 150 million kilometers.
An even greater unit of measurement is light years (ly); the distance that light travels in one year. One light year is approximately 63241 AUs. Because it's such a complicated converting process, when something is measured in light years it's usually a small, one or two digit number. With even greater distances, parallax seconds, (parsecs, pc) are used. If light years are calculated in the millions or thousands, then they are turned into parsecs. Megaparsecs are used by astronomers to calculate EVEN GREATER distances, for example as a scale for calculating distances between galaxies.
